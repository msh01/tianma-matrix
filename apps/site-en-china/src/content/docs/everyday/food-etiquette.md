---
title: "Everyday Food Etiquette in China"
description: "Simple notes on shared dishes, ordering, hosting, and polite behavior at casual meals in China."
---

Many Chinese meals are shared. Dishes are placed in the center, and people take small portions into their own bowls. This makes ordering and table behavior slightly different from individual-plate dining.

In casual settings, the host may order for the group, add dishes, or encourage guests to eat more. This is often a sign of hospitality rather than pressure.

Food etiquette varies by region and context, but understanding shared dishes, seating, and hosting behavior helps avoid most awkward moments.
